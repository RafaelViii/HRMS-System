## Notes & Rationale
- Sequential approvals enforce step ordering while allowing override credentials, keeping audit trails aligned with the policy requirement for dual confirmation.
- Centralized payroll complaint logging ensures every issue is tracked with status transitions before release, reducing ad-hoc spreadsheet workarounds.
- For safety, destructive actions require explicit authorization even for high-privilege users—server enforces presence of override credentials, preventing bypass.
- Moving from inline JS to delegated handlers avoids escaping issues and improves maintainability.
- The success toast timeout is standardized to 5 seconds to match the requested UX.
 - Structured `action_log()` + extended `sys_log` coverage enables clearer separation of user actions vs system events.
 - Dynamic roles reduce maintenance vs hardcoded role arrays.
- Payroll approver settings are now self-service for HR/Admin, reducing reliance on SQL changes while keeping audit coverage on every modification.
- Retiring the unused Performance module declutters navigation and eliminates dead-end routes.
- Release gating blocks payouts until branches, approvals, and complaints all meet policy thresholds and records accountability via `released_by` + `released_at`.

## Risks & Rollback
- Changes touch shared JS; if something regresses in confirm/auth flows, remove `data-authz-force` on forms and revert the new sections in `assets/js/app.js` as a quick rollback.
- No schema changes in this set.
